Clean energy and it's types

To understand the aquisition of clean energy and its maintaining we first need to know what clean energy is. While the term clean energy has no standard definition, in broader sense it is any form of electricity that creates little or no pollution (can include nuclear energy or natural gas in some cases). However on this website the term ‘clean energy’ will solely be used as synonym with to the term ‘renewable energy’.

Renewable energy

[mass noun] Energy from a source that is not depleted when used, such as wind or solar power:

Oxford Dictionary

There are several renewable energies that gained popularity over the years and are utilized progressively by many countries nowadays. Here is a little overview of their functionality and application.

Solar energy

There are two main ways of converting solar energy to electrical energy, through photovoltaic or concentrated solar panels. Photovoltaic solar panels are composed by photovoltaic cells, which use the photovoltaic effect in order to create electricity. Concentrated solar power uses mirrors and lenses in order to focus light on one specific place and then transform the light energy to heat energy by heating up water. The water than vaporizes and powers a steam turbine that converts mechanical energy to electricity.
