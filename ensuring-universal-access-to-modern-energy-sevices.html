Ensuring universal access to modern energy sevices

By 2030, ensure universal access to affordable, reliable and modern energy services.

United Nations website

What does it mean exactly?

By 2030, everybody should have access to energy services like power generation, energy supply and energy infrastructure outsourcing. All of these energy services should be modern, reliable and cost-effective in order to be accessed easily, even by developing or low-income countries.

Current progress toward goal

Mostly low- and middle-income countries are the ones that suffer the most from the scarcity of reliable and affordable electricity. According to the Worlds Health Organization there are around 3 billoon people that use solid fuels (wood, crop waste, coal etc.) on open stoves to cook their food ("Household indoor air pollution"). Leaving aside the enviromental impact for a moment, living like this is a major contributor to the air polution, which is a very damaging factor when it comes to the helath of people living in the area.

Health aspect

There are numerous diseases that are directly related to the polution caused by the inefficient use of solid fuels and affect predominantly children. These diseases include Pneumonia, stroke, ischaemic heart disease and lung cancer. Alltogether, "Globally, 4.3 million deaths were attributable to household air pollution (HAP) in 2012, almost all in low and middle income (LMI) countries."("Burden of Disease"). However it is not only the polution that poses significant health risk, since many of the people mentioned above use kerosone lamps for lighting and cook on open fires there is a notable fire risk along with the diseases already mentioned. This example concentrates only on one side of the problem, the unavailability of electricity for everyday cooking. However this example clearly shows the negative impacts of unaccesibility of energy sevices to country and its population.

Enviromental aspect

Access to modern, reliable and hopefuly clean energy for everybody would ensure that nebody will keep using inefficient and most of the time obsolete methods and fuels to make up for the lack of electricity. One of the main issues beingg the climate change. When using simple biomass and coal stoves, lot of the energy from the fuel is lost in incomplete combustion create products like black carbon or methan and "Black carbon (sooty particles) and methane emitted by inefficient stove combustion are powerful climate change pollutants." ("Household Air Pollution and Health."). The other major enviromental issue is deforestation. In places which don't have access to other fuels like coal and heavliy rely on wood as their primary fuel source outnumber the amount of trees that can regrow naturally which results in lost of habitat and biodiversity.

Future development of the goal

The percantage of people that have access to cooking with clean fuels and technologiesincreased from 51% in 2000 to 58% in 2014. While this is quite successful increase it is still not enough, which is why the WHO and other big companies put a lot of effort and resources to improve the percentage even further. Few out of many of the investments include provison of clean fuels like liquefied petroleum gas, or modern cooking solutions like improved stoves
