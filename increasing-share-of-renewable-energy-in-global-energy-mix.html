Increasing the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix.

By 2030, increase substantially the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix

United Nations website

What does it mean exactly?

By 2030, the amount of electricity coming from renewable energy should globally increase substantially in order to increase the percentage of renewable energy consumption compared to energy consumption from other sources worldwide.

Current progress toward goal

The more and more popular use of renewable energy, especially hydro, wind and solar is slowly increasing the share of renewable energy in the global energy consumption. From 2000’s 17,4 per cent the share of renewable energy increased to 18.1 per cent in 2012. This one percent increase over 12 years might not seem significant at the face value but when looked a little bit deeper we can analyze many different areas of the global energy mix and begin to understand how significant one percent in the global enery mix really is.

As you can see on the graph above, the German advisory council on the global environmental change predicts that from 2030 the share of renewable energy in the global mix will start to increase and will continue to do so even more rapidly after 2050. We can also see that the predcition is that the renewables will grow exponentialy with the tipping point being 2030. Despite the fact that this UN goal focuses on achiving substential increase by 2030, we can safely say that "regardless of the actual rates in the future, renewables’ exponential growth are making a sizable dent in emissions today"(Gipe) and therefore even getting one step closer (or 1 percent closer) to the mostly sustainable global energy mix is an achivevement.

Future development of the goal
